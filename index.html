About

Microbial Genomics, End to End

I specialise in designing and delivering automated AMR surveillance and phylogenomics pipelines for bacterial pathogens of clinical relevance — including Neisseria gonorrhoeae, Streptococcus pyogenes, and Klebsiella pneumoniae.

My pipelines are built with Nextflow DSL2, containerised with Docker/Singularity for portability, and paired with interactive Dash dashboards so clinicians and epidemiologists can explore results without touching the command line.

My work is designed for public health labs and research groups who need rigorous, reproducible genomic workflows — from assembly QC through resistance profiling, typing, and phylogenetic inference.

Neisseria gonorrhoeae
AMR Diagnostic Pipeline
GitHub

End-to-end Nextflow DSL2 pipeline characterising antimicrobial resistance across 283 clinical strains. Integrates AMRFinderPlus resistance profiling with a custom alignment-based mutation caller (penA, gyrA, parC, mtrR, porB) and promoter-variant detection, core-genome phylogeny via Parsnp, and an interactive Dash dashboard linking resistance phenotypes to phylogenetic context for rapid clinical interpretation.

Klebsiella pneumoniae
Phylogenomics Pipeline
GitHub

Comprehensive phylogenomics pipeline spanning the full analysis chain for 150 K. pneumoniae genomes. Assembly QC (QUAST), AMR and resistance gene profiling (AMRFinderPlus), virulence and typing (Kleborate), SNP-based variant calling (Snippy), recombination masking (Gubbins), and maximum-likelihood phylogeny (IQ-TREE). Results delivered via a six-tab Dash dashboard. ST11 identified as the dominant lineage, consistent with global carbapenem-resistant K. pneumoniae trends.
